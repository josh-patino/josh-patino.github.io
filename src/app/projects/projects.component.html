Senior Project: Counting Seals

This was my Senior CS project. The premise was that our client was a contracted researcher for USC, helping count seals during mating season with drones off the coast of California. My specific role was to create a stitched image (we were given hundreds, all at 4K resolution) to increase image processing with our ML algorithm. We estimated that a stitched image reduced processing speeds by about 30%, and the image quality was important to maintain since pups (baby seals) look almost identical to the sand. The image on the left is 179MB! I created the stitching algorithm in Python using the cv2 image library. It was a lot of fun, as I got to work with a client for the first time. Unfortunately, I did not get to see seals up close, but maybe next time!
